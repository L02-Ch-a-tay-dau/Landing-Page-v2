1. IDEA PHASE
Why does this product exist?

Problem
We address three major pain points in the daily dining experience:

Decision Fatigue: The average person spends 15-20 minutes scrolling through delivery apps without making a choice.
Choice Overload: Existing apps display hundreds of options at once in a list format, causing cognitive overload and stress.
Social Friction: "What should we eat?" is a common source of conflict for couples and groups, often leading to frustration or uneven compromises.
Solution
FoodMatch streamlines the decision-making process with three key features:

Swipe-to-Choose Interface: An intuitive mechanism where users simply "Swipe Right" to like a dish or "Swipe Left" to pass. A match happens only when everyone in the group swipes right.
Integrated Navigation: Once a place is matched, the app provides instant, turn-by-turn directions to the restaurant via Google Maps or Apple Maps integration.
Curated "Best-Of" Recommendations: Instead of overwhelming users with every option, our AI filters and suggests only top-rated restaurants and local favorites based on current location and preferences.
Unique Value Proposition
Time Efficiency: Reduces decision time from ~20 minutes to under 5 minutes.
Gamification: Transforms a tedious chore into an engaging, interactive game.
Fairness: Eliminates bias; the final choice is mathematically guaranteed to be acceptable to everyone.
2. SELL PHASE
How we reach the market and compete.

Advantage
Gamified UX: Unlike Google Maps or Yelp (which are static directories), our app provides an entertaining experience.
Competitive Pricing: Our premium subscription is priced significantly lower than competitors, making it accessible for students and young couples.
Negative Data Utilization: We capture what users don't like to refine recommendations—data that competitors rarely utilize effectively.
Customer Segments
Primary: Gen Z and Millennials (Ages 18-35) in urban areas who dine out or order food frequently.
Secondary: Office workers looking for quick consensus on lunch spots.
Niche: "Foodies" who want to discover new places but are overwhelmed by options.
Key Metrics
Match Rate: The percentage of sessions that result in a successful restaurant match.
DAU (Daily Active Users): Frequency of app usage per day.
Retention Rate: The percentage of users who return to the app for their next meal decision.
3. BUSINESS MODEL
How we operate and generate revenue.

Channels
Viral Content: Leveraging TikTok and Instagram Reels with relatable content about "indecisive couples."
Referral Program: Incentivizing users to invite partners/friends to unlock "Super Likes" or premium features.
ASO (App Store Optimization): Targeting high-intent keywords like "food decider," "couple apps," and "restaurant finder."
Cost Structure
Cloud Infrastructure: Hosting costs (AWS/Firebase) for real-time socket connections.
Data APIs: Licensing fees for restaurant data (e.g., Google Places API, Yelp Fusion) – this is the primary operational cost.
User Acquisition: Paid marketing on social platforms to drive initial downloads.
Revenue Streams
Affiliate Commissions: Earning a fee when users click "Order Now" and are redirected to delivery partners (GrabFood, ShopeeFood, UberEats).
Native Advertising: Restaurants pay for "Sponsored Cards" to appear more frequently in the swipe stack (labeled as Promoted).
Freemium Model: * Free: Basic swiping with ads.
