Project Overview
1. Project Name
Tinner (A portmanteau of "Tinder" - the swiping mechanism, and "Dinner" - the meal)

2. Slogan
"Swipe To Bite"

3. Project Purpose
The Problem (The Pain Point)
In modern life, the question "What should we eat today?" has become a daily obsession (Decision Fatigue) for students and office workers:

Choice Matrix (Overload): Current food delivery apps (ShopeeFood, Grab) display lists of hundreds of restaurants in a scrollable format, overwhelming users with information.
Time-Consuming: On average, users spend 15-20 minutes just scrolling and debating, often leading to choosing a random unsatisfactory dish or skipping the meal entirely.
The Paradox of Choice: The more options available, the harder it is for people to make a decision, and the less satisfied they are with that decision.
Tinner's Solution
Tinner solves the "Analysis Paralysis" problem by simplifying the decision-making process to the maximum:

Single-View Interface: Only displays one dish at a time. Users are not distracted by other options.
Gamified Experience (Gamification): Applies the "Swipe Left (Pass) - Swipe Right (Like)" mechanism. This turns stressful food selection into a fun and engaging game.
Product Goals
Solve the "Paradox of Choice": Help users go from opening the App to deciding on a meal in under 5 minutes.
Visual-first Experience: Build an interface focused on food imagery to stimulate the appetite ("Eat with your eyes").
Complete MVP (Minimum Viable Product): Focus on implementing 2 core features that fully resolve the main User Flow:
Swipe-to-Match Logic: Users swipe left (pass) or right (like) on food images. Upon liking a dish, the application immediately reveals the corresponding restaurant that serves it.
Restaurant Info Card: Visually displays detailed information about the matched restaurant, including food images, address, opening hours, and basic details so users can make their final decision.
User Experience (UX): Build a "Visual-first" interface that is intuitive and easy to use for non-tech-savvy users.
